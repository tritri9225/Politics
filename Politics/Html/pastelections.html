Past Elections

Looking at past election data is important for understanding trends and predicting future election results. The 2016 presidential election gives us a lot of useful information, especially since two of the candidates are running again for 2020. The democratic candidates need to look at which red states from 2016 they can win over in 2020, and the opposite for republicans. On this page, you will find graphs from 2016 that give insight into how voters may behave in 2020, as well as statistics and information to further comprehend politics in the US.

This graph shows the total number of votes that Donald Trump and Bernie Sanders received in the 2016 primary election. From this graph, we know the total amount of votes, but not which states turned out to be red or blue. The candidates can see approximately how many votes they would need to beat their opponent, but not which states to target for additional support.

This map represents Trump's popularity by county in the 2016 primary election. This visualization is more helpful when telling which states have more republican voters than others. Still, you can notice that there are huge gaps of white space in the map. This does not mean that Trump did not get any votes in these areas; the data used to make these graphs did not contain every single county in America. Some were left out, but you are still able to generally tell which states are more likely to vote for a republican candidate than others. Click on the map for a link to interact with it!
